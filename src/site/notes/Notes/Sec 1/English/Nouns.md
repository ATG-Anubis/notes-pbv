-   What are nouns?
    
    Nouns are the **names** of things around us.
    
-   What are common nouns?
    
    They are nouns that are used to name general things
    
    Examples: dog, table, car, bottle
    
-   What are proper nouns?
    
    They are nouns that name a particular or special person or thing
    
    Examples: Mr. Rogers, San Francisco, July
    
-   How do we write proper nouns?
    
    With a capital letter at the beginning
    
-   What do we call nouns that are used for a collection of things?
    
    Collective nouns
    
    Examples: a **flock** of birds, a **bunch** of grapes, a **pod** of dolphins, a **school** of fish
    
-   Why can it sometimes be difficult to decide whether a collective noun is singular or plural?
    
    -   because words that represent a number of different things in the same group always take a singular verb
        -   Examples: furniture is, luggage is, linen is
    -   Words that have a plural meaning but no plural form take a plural verb
        -   Examples: people are, police are
    -   Other collective nouns can take a plural verb if the context emphasis is on a group of individuals. However, the singular is preferred
        -   Examples: The crowd is cheering, The crew is ready, The Olympic Games are in Salt Lake City
-   What are the two forms of the noun?
    
    Singular
    
    Plural
    
-   What are the plural constructions?
    
    -   Many plurals are made by simply adding -s
        -   Examples: dog/dogs, girl/girls
    -   If the noun ends in -s, -sh, -ch, or -x, make the plural by adding -es
        -   Examples: bus/buses, bush/bushes, fox/foxes, church/churches
    -   If the noun ends in a -y before a consonant, change -y to -i and add -es to make the plural
        -   Examples: fairy/fairies, city/cities
    -   if the noun ends in a -y before a vowel, add -s to make the plural
        -   Examples: Monkey/monkeys, toy/toys
    -   if the noun ends in -f, change the -f to a -v and add -es
        -   Examples: loaf/loaves, leaf/leaves
    -   Some nouns are irregular
        -   Examples: foot/feet, man/men, child/children
-   How do you form the possessive of a singular noun?
    
    -   By adding an apostrophe and -s at the end of the word
        -   Examples: the boy’s dog, the lady’s car
    -   The possessive of a plural noun ending in -s is formed by adding an apostrophe
        -   Examples: horses’ manes, ladies’ cars
-   What are terms of address?
    
    They are nouns we use when we refer to or address certain people
    
    Examples: Mr. Jones, Doctor Smith, Captain Peters
    
-   What are abstract nouns?
    
    It’s the name of something that can’t be recognized by the 5 senses, but only by the mind
    
    Examples: courage, misery, excitement, hope, possibility
